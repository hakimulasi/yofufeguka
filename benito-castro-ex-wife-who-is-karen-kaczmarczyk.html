    Born in Mexico City on June 5, 1946, Arturo Benito Castro Hernández had a career of almost five decades in television, theater, and music in Mexico, his role being “El Papiringo” in the comedy series “La Güereja y algo more” one of his most emblematic, in addition to being in “La Carabina de Ambrosio” in the 80s.

    He maintained a close friendship with the late Paco Stanley, a television host who was murdered in 1999 for alleged links to organized crime. In a recent documentary, Benito Castro confessed that he and Stanley took drugs together, and even claimed that he supplied him with narcotics, in addition to having met some cartel leaders.

    In the 1960s, he joined the famous musical group Los Hermanos Castro when they were working in Las Vegas. It was decided that Benito would join the group to play guitar and sing harmonies, but he also became popular with the public for his comedic interventions on stage.
