    Meet Andrew Form, Film Producer, And Alexandra Daddarios Husband

    Andrew Form is a successful American film producer who has played a significant role in the making of several blockbuster movies like The Purge, Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, and A Quiet Place.

    Andrew Form is a successful American film producer who has played a significant role in the making of several blockbuster movies like “The Purge,” “Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles,” and “A Quiet Place.”

    Andrew Form is also known as the ex-husband of Jordana Brewster and the husband of the famous American film actress Alexandra Daddario.

    He has established himself as a prominent figure in the industry, particularly horror and action, by reimagining classic franchises and exploring contemporary themes in thrilling narratives.

    Profile Summary

    Biography

    Andrew Form is 54 and was born in New York City, New York, on February 3, 1969. Although there isn’t much information available about his early life, it is known that he grew up in Los Angeles, probably surrounded by the world of film and entertainment.

    His love for movies began at an early age, and he was inspired by the works of famous filmmakers like Steven Spielberg, George Lucas, and James Cameron.

    Education

    Although specific information about Andrew’s formal education is not widely available, it is known that he didn’t attend a traditional film school.

    Instead, he gained practical experience and immersed himself in the industry through various avenues. Andrew began his career as a production assistant for renowned producer Jerry Bruckheimer, where he worked on several films such as “The Rock,” “Con Air,” and “Enemy of the State.”

    This hands-on experience exposed him to the inner workings of film production. It provided invaluable mentorship from a Hollywood veteran.

    Andrew then built upon his experience by working directly with Michael Bay on major films like “Armageddon” and “Pearl Harbor.” This close collaboration further honed his skills and understanding of blockbuster filmmaking.

    Despite failing to follow the traditional educational path, Andrew’s early immersion in the industry and direct learning from industry leaders proved instrumental in shaping him into the successful producer he is today.

    Career

    Andrew Form’s career in the entertainment industry has been defined by grit, creativity, and strategic partnerships, taking him from a production assistant to a leading Hollywood producer.

    He started working under industry legends like Jerry Bruckheimer and Michael Bay, gaining invaluable experience in major blockbuster films.

    In 1995, Form co-founded the production company Platinum Dunes with Bay and Brad Fuller, focusing on reimagining classic horror franchises.

    Their first major success was the 2003 remake of “The Texas Chainsaw Massacre,” for which Form served as an executive producer. He revived the genre and established Platinum Dunes as a significant player.

    He continued achieving horror hits with remakes of “Friday the 13th,” “A Nightmare on Elm Street,” “Ouija: Origin of Evil,” “The Unborn,” “The Hitcher,” and “The Amityville Horror.”

    Beyond horror, Form branched into action films like “Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles” and its sequels and sci-fi flicks like “Project Almanac.”

    He explored thought-provoking dystopian themes in the “Purge” franchise and its spin-off series while achieving critical acclaim for the suspenseful “A Quiet Place” films.

    Expanding into television, he served as executive producer on the historical pirate drama “Black Sails” and the film adaptation of “The Last Ship.” He also produced the 2020 film “Songbird,” one of the first films to be made during the COVID-19 pandemic.

    He is currently involved with the Amazon series “The Terminal List.” Underpinning Form’s diverse productions is a collaborative spirit, genre versatility, commercial savvy, and philanthropic focus.

    His willingness to evolve and take calculated risks has shaped his journey from a fledgling film worker to an esteemed Hollywood producer. In 2015, he was named one of the “30 Most Powerful Film Producers in Hollywood” by The Hollywood Reporter.

    Form’s career inspires those looking to make their mark in the competitive entertainment industry through dedication, creativity, and a nuanced understanding of audiences.

    Family

    Andrew Form values his family life and finds strength and happiness in his close bonds with his loved ones.

    Here is a glimpse into his family circle:

    Parents

    Andrew Form grew up supported by devoted parents, Fred and Joann Form. His father, Fred, attended his son’s little league baseball games and school events, cultivating a close father-son bond from a young age.

    Though Fred’s profession is not widely known, he took pride in Andrew’s budding Hollywood career.

    Meanwhile, Andrew’s mother, Joann, was the cornerstone of the Form family, unconditionally loving her children and teaching them strong values through her own warmth, kindness, and work ethic.

    Both parents laid the foundation that helped guide Andrew into the principled producer he became.

    Wife

    In June of 2022, Andrew tied the knot with actress Alexandra Daddario, with whom he had been since 2020. The beautiful ceremony took place in New Orleans.

    Alexandra is most famous for her significant roles in movies such as Percy Jackson, San Andreas, Baywatch, and Hall Pass. Despite her busy schedule, she makes time for her advocacy work, which speaks volumes about her nurturing and driven personality.

    Andrew and Alexandra are an ideal power couple whose shared success strengthens their bond.

    Children

    Andrew has two sons from his previous marriage. Julian, born in 2013, and Rowan, born just three years later in 2016, share a close bond with their dad.

    They often join him on the red carpet for his premieres and accompany him on casual outings like grabbing ice cream or playing baseball, cherishing every moment they spend together.

    As a hands-on dad, Andrew can balance his professional ambitions with always making time for his children.

    Extended family and friends

    Andrew has a close relationship with his extended family and friends. He regards Alexandra’s sister as his stepdaughter and bonds with his wider family.

    He also values friendships with professional collaborators such as Michael Bay and Brad Fuller and enjoys blending work and personal community.

    Andrew places a high priority on family and values strong relationships in all aspects of life. His parents have been important role models for him, and he adores his children. These connections serve as pillars of support for him.
