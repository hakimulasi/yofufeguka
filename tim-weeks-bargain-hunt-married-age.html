    Tim Weeks spent his early life surrounded by antiques and the thrill of the auction house. Being raised by an auctioneer father, he watched his dad carry the gavel at auctions in Badminton and Grittleton before he set up Wessex Auction Rooms in Draycot Cerne.

    Tim Weeks is an English auctioneer, cataloger, director, valuer, and television personality. He is best known for the long-haul BBC One program named Bargain Hunt.

    Tim Weeks Bio & Family; Age, Father, Ethnicity

    Probably into his mid-30s, Tim Weeks was born somewhere around the 1980s in Chippenham, England. Besides a British nationality, Tim possesses white ethnicity.

    Tim’s father, Peter Weeks, is his biggest inspiration when it comes to his choice to become a professional auctioneer. He spent his weekends as a six-year-old sitting in the corner of auction rooms watching his father wield the gavel.

    The famed auctioneer grew up in a house where the dinner table discussions were based on the items that had been brought in that day. Eventually, that’s how Tim got more insights into auctioneering at an early age.

    His Career & TV Shows: Bargain Hunt

    Tim Weeks is the proud co-owner, valuer, and auctioneer of a company called Wessex Auction Rooms. He and his business partner, Martin Hughes, took over the company back in January 2016, which was initially formed by his auctioneer father.

    Aside from running an auction house, Tim also serves as a radio host. Back in 2016, he landed a gig as the co-presenter of a series on the BBC One network called Street Auction. Similarly, he serves as a regular expert on the BBC One show, Bargain Hunt.

    Apart from that, Tim hosts a show on the BBC Wiltshire radio network and is also the presenter of his own segment named TheSocial. In addition to being an entrepreneur, radio host, and full-time bargain hunter, Tim contributes regularly to various blogs, magazines, and websites.
