    Paden Bray- Professional Career

    Paden has been involved in the equestrian sport of rodeo since the age of eight. While in junior high, he was the reserve champion team roping heeler at the National Junior High Rodeo. Paden then competed in The American in 2017 while still in high school, and he also qualified for the National high school championship rodeo the same year. He gained a lot of expertise and passion for the sport, and he chose to make it his job. Paden became a tie-down roper and heeler for his collegiate rodeo team. In the year 2019, he joined the Professional Rodeo Cowboys. Paden was named the rookie rodeo cowboy of the year in Heeling in 2019 and finished fourth in the Wrangler Nationals Final Rodeo qualification the same year.

    He finished his rookie season in 17th place in the PRCA, heeling behind 2017 world champion Header Erich Rodgers. Paden Bray began the 2020 PRCA Pro Rodeo season in 11th place. He also won his first-ever NFR finals with Erich Rodger in December 2020. This season, he has placed third in rodeos such as the San Antonio Stock Show Rodeo, the World’s Oldest Rodeo, the Cody Stampede, and the Guymon Pioneer Days rodeo. He and Erich Rodgers recently competed in The American Rodeo at AT&T Stadium, where they won the team Roping title and Rodgers was among the other winners.
