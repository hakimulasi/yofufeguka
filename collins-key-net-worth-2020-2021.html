    Career

    When he was 16 years old, he was discovered on YouTube by the producers of AGT, and he was then invited to appear in the 8th season of the show. He gained popularity very quickly, and he was the first-ever magician to go into the final. He is often called “Magic’s First Pop Star.” In 2013 he toured across the country with America’s Got Talent, and he visited 36 cities in six weeks.

    After AGT, he focused on his YouTube Channel. He was able to grow his YouTube channel from 2 million to 18 million subscribers in just two years, and each episode has been averaging 25 million views over the last two years. He and his brother Devan mainly upload advertiser-friendly comedy videos, and thus he has partnered with many advertisers like Unilever, Johnson & Johnson, and Disney. He also created The Disappearing Girl, who applauded by the audience a lot, and it was his first show to win a Shorty Award, an AdWeek Arch Award, a Cannes Lions Awards, and also a Streamy award.

    In 2017, he was given a chance to create and produce six weeks of interstitial content for The Disney Channel Television Network, and this lead to the Fall Season Friday Night line-up. He also gave a TEDx talk in 2017 at the University of Santa Barbara, and the topic was related to magic and how to use it to solve your problems. He later headlined in the YouTube fanfest concert in Mumbai in India, and the show was attended by more than 10,000 fans.
