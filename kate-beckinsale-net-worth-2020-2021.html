    Early Life

    Kate Beckinsale was born on July 26, 1973, in Chiswick, London, England. She was born to Richard Beckinsale and Judy Loe who are both actors. She is their only child. Her father died at the age of 31; he died of a heart attack. She was only five years old at that time.

    Her mother moved in with director Roy Battersby. Roy had four sons and a daughter. Kate attended a girl’s school called Godolphin and Latymer School in Hammersmith, West London. She attended New College, Oxford, where she read French and Russian Literature. She travelled to Paris as a requirement for modern language students.

    In 1995, she left the university to concentrate on her acting career. According to her, the pressure from both acting and schooling was too high; she could not enjoy either of them because of the pressure.

    Personal Life

    Kate Beckinsale dated actor Michael Sheen from 1995 to 2003. They have a daughter named Lily. She was born in 1999. She got married to director Len Wiseman in 2004. They divorced in 2016.

    Age, Height, and Weight

    Career

    Kate Beckinsale made her film debut in 1991 with a small role in “Devices and Desires”. In the same year, she appeared in the film titled “One Against the Wind”. In 1993, she landed a major role in the film titled “Much Ado About Nothing”. In 1994, she starred in “Prince of Jutland” and “Cold Comfort Farm” in 1995. She later starred in Jane Austen’s “Emma” and another film titled “Shooting Fish”.

    She moved to Hollywood in 1998. She starred in “The Last Days of Disco” and “Through the Looking Glass” in the same year. In the year 2000, she appeared in “The Golden Bowl”. Her popularity increased after she starred in the 2001 war film titled “Pearl Harbor”, she played the role of a nurse torn between two pilots. She also appeared in the romantic comedy movie “Serendipity” in the same year.

    Kate Beckinsale appeared in 2003’s “Underworld”. She played the role of a vampire. A role that was totally different from her usual romantic comedy and period films. In 2004, she appeared in “The Aviator” and horror movie “Van Helsing”.

    She also reprised her role as Selene in 2006’s “Underworld: Evolution”. The movie was directed by her husband, and she was involved in the entire process. Her daughter, Lily, appeared in the movie as young Selene. In 2007, she appeared in a thriller titled “Vacancy”, she starred alongside Luke Wilson.

    She starred in “Winged Creatures” in 2008. In the same year, she appeared in “Nothing but the Truth”. She appeared in several other movies including “Absolutely Anything” and 2016 romantic comedy titled “Love and Friendship”. She has also worked as a model occasionally.

    Awards & Achievements

    Kate Beckinsale won the Best Actress for her performance in “Shooting Fish” at the 1997 Sitges – Catalan International Film Festival. She also won the British Supporting Actress of the Year for her role in “The Last Days of Disco” at the 1999 London Critics Award.

    She also won another award at the London Critics Award in 2017. Kate has also been nominated for several other awards including the Gotham Awards.
