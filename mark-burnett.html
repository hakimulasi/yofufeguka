    Mark Burnett Facts

  • He had joined the British Army when he was 17 years old. He had risen through the ranks and become a Section Commander in the Parachute Regiment. Between 1978 and 1982, he had served with the 3rd Battalion, Parachute Regiment, and had been involved on the frontline during the Falklands War.
  • In 1982, he had emigrated to the United States in October that year. A friend of his named Nick Hill, who had emigrated earlier from the UK, had helped him secure a job as a live-in nanny and security guard with an affluent family in Beverly Hills.
  • A few years after working in a couple of similar jobs, he had rented a portion of a fence at Venice Beach in Los Angeles where he used to sell T-shirts during weekends.
  • He had entered, in 1991, a French adventure race-cum-competition called Raid Gauloises. He identified a new business opportunity in this, purchased the format’s rights, and launched a similar show known as Eco-Challenge. This was his debut as a TV producer.
  • As of October 2020, TV series produced by him had been nominated 143 times at the Emmy Awards. At a personal level, he had won 12 Emmy Awards, 5 Producers Guild of America Awards, and 7 Critics’ Choice Television Awards by then.
